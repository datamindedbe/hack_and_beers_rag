# Hiroshi Tanaka

## Education
Hiroshi's education was a lifelong apprenticeship under his father, Master Koji. He began learning the art of bonsai as a young boy, eventually attending the Tokyo School of Bonsai to refine his skills and deepen his theoretical knowledge.

## Work History

### Master Bonsai Artist (2010-Present)
As the current patriarch of the Tanaka family tradition, Hiroshi:
- Manages his family's celebrated gardens
- Provides meticulous care and cultivation of bonsai trees, some hundreds of years old
- Works on private commissions for wealthy clients and museums
- Creates stunning new pieces while preserving ancient traditions

### Apprentice & Lead Artist (1990-2010)
Worked for various private collections:
- Built his reputation under the guidance of other masters
- Honed his craft through dedicated practice
- Learned from diverse bonsai traditions

## Skills & Passions
For Hiroshi, bonsai is a spiritual practice, not just a job.

### Technical Mastery
- Master of bonsai cultivation
- Expert in tree sculpting and shaping
- Deep knowledge of soil composition
- Understanding of horticultural science

### Philosophical Approach
- Profound patience and calm demeanor
- Deep connection to the natural world
- Passionate about preserving and sharing this ancient art form
- Believes bonsai teaches patience, respect for nature, and the beauty of quiet strength

His work represents a harmonious blend of artistic skill, scientific knowledge, and spiritual dedication.
